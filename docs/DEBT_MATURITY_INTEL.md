## 1. Total CRE Debt Maturing 2026–2028

| Year | MBA Estimate | S&P Global / Trepp | MSCI |
|------|-------------|---------------------|------|
| **2025** | $957B (actual) | $950B (actual) | ~$930B+ |

| **2027** | **$652B** | **$1.26T (PEAK YEAR)** | Peak year |

17% of the $5.0 trillion in outstanding commercial mortgages is scheduled to mature in 2026 (MBA, February 2026). The S&P Global $1.26T peak in 2027 includes extension rollovers from 2024/2025.

## 3. CMBS Spreads — Current vs. Historical

**Current conduit CMBS spreads (early 2026):**

| Tranche | Current Spread (bps) |
|---------|---------------------|
| Conduit AAA | +75 |
| Conduit A-S | +105 |
| Conduit AA | +125 |
| Conduit A | +175 |
| Conduit BBB- | +435 |
| SASB AAA | +100 to +135 |
| CRE CLO AAA (static/managed) | +135 / +140 |
| CRE CLO BBB- (static/managed) | +275 / +285 |

Spreads are "already very tight" relative to historical norms — notable given elevated distress. This disconnect reflects strong investor appetite for senior tranches amid yield hunger. During the GFC (2009), conduit AAA spreads blew out to 1,000+ bps.

YTD 2026 private-label CMBS and CRE CLO issuance: **$15.4 billion**, up 15% from $13.3B in same period 2025.

## 5. Bank CRE Exposure

**1,374 banks** (31% of all FDIC-insured institutions) exceed the 300% CRE-to-Tier 1 capital concentration threshold at year-end 2024.

Among the 158 largest U.S. banks, **59 face exposures greater than 300%** of Tier 1 capital. The broader universe of nearly 2,000 banks with elevated CRE exposure is cited as a systemic risk.

- Community bank median CRE concentration ratio: **195%** (up ~1ppt from year-end 2023)
- PDNA rates for non-owner-occupied CRE and multifamily remain well above pre-pandemic averages
- Capital rule changes effective April 1, 2026 primarily benefit GSIBs, not community banks

## 6. Key Distressed Deals / Notable Defaults (2025–2026)

### Brookfield
- Ceded control or sold at discount on assets once valued at **$3B+**
- $2.8B office debt due 2025, **$5.3B due 2026**
- Six Maryland suburban office buildings set for foreclosure auction ($223M loan, matured August 2023, unpaid)
- Once held 93M sqft valued at $33.5B
- Simultaneously raised **$16B distressed opportunity fund** (record close)

### Blackstone
- Wrote down 1.3M sqft Chicago office (acquired $378M in 2015, $310M CMBS loan) **to zero**
- Handed keys back on Manhattan tower (acquired $605M in 2014); special servicer sold $308M loan for $200M — **first AAA CMBS tranche loss since GFC**

### Columbia Property Trust
- Default on **$1.7B loan** (balance climbed past $1.9B) backed by 7 office towers including 650 California St., SF

### SL Green / RXR — Worldwide Plaza
- Goldman Sachs, Deutsche Bank, and others filed **$940M foreclosure lawsuit** against Manhattan tower

### Starwood REIT (SREIT)
- NAV down 40% from 2022 peak to **$8.36B**
- Monthly withdrawals capped at 0.5% NAV (1.5%/quarter)
- $850M pending withdrawal requests
- Minimum investor net worth raised to $350K from $250K (January 2026)

### Market-Wide
- Nearly **150 CRE foreclosures** in H1 2025 — highest midyear total since 2014
- More than half of $100B in CMBS office loans maturing 2026 unlikely to pay off at maturity
- 345 office loans ($13.72B) due by year-end 2026; $2.38B have DSCR of 1.09x or less

## 10. Federal Reserve / Regulatory Actions

### 2026 Stress Test (finalized February 2026)
- Severely adverse scenario: **39–40% decline in CRE prices**
- Comparable to 2021–2024 test severity
- Unemployment rises to 10% peak
- CRE, BBB spreads, stock prices set toward "more severe half" of range
- First time Fed sought public comment on scenarios before finalizing

### Ongoing Supervisory Focus
- Fed warning of CRE risks for community and regional banks
- Reviewing loan practices and credit loss reserves
- Congressional Research Service report (R48175) documents CRE-banking nexus

### Capital Rule Changes (effective April 1, 2026)
- eSLR modifications reduce Tier 1 requirements for GSIBs by ~2% ($13B aggregate)
- Community Bank Leverage Ratio framework revised
- Does NOT loosen CRE concentration guidance for community banks
